# 5 Common Resume Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

Your resume might be packed with incredible experience, but a few simple mistakes can send it straight to the 'no' pile. Here are the top five resume mistakes and how you can fix them.

## 1. Typos and Grammatical Errors

This is the most common and most avoidable mistake. It signals a lack of attention to detail to potential employers.

- The Fix: Proofread your resume multiple times. Use a grammar checker, read it out loud, and have a friend review it. Even one tiny error can be a deal-breaker.

## 2. Being Too Vague

"Responsible for marketing tasks" tells a recruiter almost nothing. You need to be specific about what you did and the impact you made.

- The Fix: Quantify your achievements whenever possible. Instead of "Managed a team," try "Led a team of 5 engineers to deliver a project 3 weeks ahead of schedule, saving the company $50,000."

## 3. A One-Size-Fits-All Approach

Recruiters can spot a generic resume from a mile away. It shows a lack of genuine interest in the specific role you're applying for.

- The Fix: Tailor your resume for every single application. Use the job description as your guide and highlight the skills and experiences that directly match the company's needs.

## 5. Including Irrelevant Information

Your resume is not your life story. Including your high school job (unless you're a very recent graduate) or unrelated hobbies just wastes valuable space.

- The Fix: Be ruthless. If a piece of information doesn't support your case for *this specific job*, get rid of it. Keep your resume focused, relevant, and powerful.
