# Craft a Standout Resume for 2026

_Updated April 2026. The page slug stays the same so bookmarks and external links keep working._

The job market is more competitive than ever. Your resume is your first, and often only, chance to make a stellar impression. With recruiters spending an average of just seven seconds on each resume, every detail counts. Hereâ€™s how to make those seconds work for you.

## 1. Tailor Your Resume for Every Application

A generic resume is a fast track to the rejection pile. To catch a recruiter's eye, you need to customize your resume for each specific job you apply for.

- Analyze the Job Description: Scour the posting for keywords related to skills, responsibilities, and qualifications.
- Mirror the Language: Use the same terminology as the job description. This shows you've done your homework and are a strong fit. ## 2. Start with a Powerful Professional Summary

Think of your professional summary as your 30-second elevator pitch. It should be a concise, 2-4 sentence overview of your career, skills, and goals.

- Weak Example: "Experienced professional looking for a new opportunity."
- Strong Example: "Results-driven Software Engineer with 5+ years of experience in developing, testing, and maintaining scalable web applications. Proficient in React, Node.js, and cloud-native technologies, seeking to leverage my skills to build innovative solutions at a forward-thinking tech company."

> !TIP Your summary is your hook. Read our deep dive on [writing a professional summary](/blog/writing-professional-summary) to master the art of the career pitch.

## 3. Quantify Your Accomplishments with Action Verbs

Don't just list your dutiesâ€”showcase your impact. Use strong action verbs and back up your achievements with numbers, percentages, or dollar amounts.

- Instead of: "Managed social media accounts."
- Write This: "Grew social media engagement by 45% across three platforms by developing and executing a data-driven content strategy."

## 4. Optimize for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

Most large companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes. To get past the bots:

- Use Standard Section Headers: Stick to clear headings like "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills."
- Include Keywords: Sprinkle relevant keywords from the job description throughout your resume.
- Avoid Complex Formatting: Choose clean, professional layouts. FitCV [templates](/templates) use ATS-friendly structuresâ€”then keep headings simple in the editor. Learn more in [how to beat the ATS bots](/blog/how-to-beat-ats).

> !TIP Use a standard reverse-chronological layout. It's the most parsed format by recursive algorithms and the most preferred by recruiters worldwide.

## 5. Keep it Clean and Readable

The design of your resume is just as important as the content. It should be easy to read and visually appealing.

- Font: Pick a professional font like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman, in a size between 10-12 points.
- White Space: Use plenty of white space to give your content room to breathe and avoid a cluttered look.
- Length: Aim for one page if you have less than 10 years of experience. Two pages is acceptable for more seasoned professionals.
