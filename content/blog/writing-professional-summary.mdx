# How to Write a Killer Professional Summary

A professional summary is a 2-4 sentence section at the top of your resume that acts as a snapshot of your career. It's your "elevator pitch" and your first chance to convince a recruiter to keep reading. Get it right, and you'll make an immediate impact.

## The 3 Key Ingredients of a Great Summary

A powerful summary should quickly answer three questions for the recruiter:

1.  Who are you? (Your professional title and years of experience)
2.  What are your key skills? (Your main areas of expertise that match the job)
3.  What's your career goal? (What you want to do next, tailored to the specific role)

## A Simple Formula for Success

[Your Title] with [# of years] of experience in [Your Industry/Field]. Skilled in [2-3 Key Skills]. Seeking to leverage my expertise to [Your Goal, aligned with the company's needs].

## Examples in Action

### For a Software Engineer

> "Detail-oriented Software Engineer with 8+ years of experience designing and building scalable, high-performance web applications. Expert in front-end development with React and TypeScript, with a proven track record of improving user experience and application performance. Eager to contribute to a fast-paced, innovative team dedicated to creating cutting-edge user interfaces."

### For a Marketing Manager

> "Data-driven Marketing Manager with a decade of experience leading successful multi-channel campaigns for B2B SaaS companies. Expertise in SEO, content marketing, and demand generation, with a history of increasing qualified leads by over 200%. Seeking to apply my strategic and analytical skills to drive growth at a mission-driven organization."

### For a Recent Graduate

> "Enthusiastic and highly-motivated recent Computer Science graduate from [University Name] with a passion for mobile development. Possess hands-on experience building iOS applications with Swift and SwiftUI through academic projects and a summer internship. Eager to apply my technical skills and collaborative mindset to a junior developer role."
